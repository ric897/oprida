Our Approach

Pre-Production Planning & Documentation

Before writing a single line of code, we make sure all due diligence is done, plans are made, and technical documentation is approved. This way both the founder(s) and development team are on the same page from the start.

Here's a deeper look at just some of the planning documents we create for our clients. For the sake of confidentiality, we'll use The ABC Company, a fictional company that wants to build an online coffee marketplace as an example.

Technical Requirements Document

Our technical requirements document outlines the specific functionalities and features the product must have. For "The ABC Company", this could involve user registration, product listings, and a checkout process. This document serves as a guidepost ensuring all necessary components are implemented.

Third-Party APIs Specification Document

For products that rely on external tools or platforms, the Third-Party APIs Specification document lists all APIs that will be integrated. For our coffee marketplace example, this could include payment gateways, shipping calculators, or customer review systems. It ensures smooth interfacing with external solutions.

Database Model & Schema

Our database model and schema layout the structure of the database, including tables, relationships, and key data points. For "The ABC Company", this would involve tables for users, products, orders, and reviews. It's vital for ensuring data integrity and efficient querying.

Sprint Planning & Ticket Creation

Sprint planning and ticket creation involve breaking down tasks into manageable units, prioritizing them, and assigning them to development sprints. For our coffee marketplace, this could start with setting up the backend, then focusing on frontend features. It ensures systematic and timely project progress.
